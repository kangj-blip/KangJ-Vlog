Title: 100 Different Questions to Stimulate the mind
---

When I was younger, I would wonder about reckless, frankly useless things, like "Why is Wi-Fi affected by harsh wind conditions?", and "When we use soap, does the soap get dirty?" Of course, the answer is no. 

But nowadays I find myself wondering about more existential, hopefully more thought-provoking questions about the mind, my relationships, nature, and self. 

Here are many such questions, where I will come back, update, answer, and delve deeper as time goes on.

1. What does it mean to truly know someone? At what point can I declare myself someone's true friend, or someone who understands them to the deepest of [levels](https://www.daniellebernock.com/what-it-means-to-know-someone-37/)? When the human mind can't understand the individualistic identity of who they themselves are, how is it possible to "know" others? How can I best allow others to "know" me?
I could know all the details people's lives, from where they were born to what their favourite car model is. But I am not satisfied with impersonal knowledge. It is all about [how we define "knowing"](https://thoughtcatalog.com/corinda-katz/2014/03/how-do-you-know-when-you-really-know-someone/).
How does this connect with celebrity culture in modern day, where people think they "know" their favourite celebrity to unsafe degrees?
